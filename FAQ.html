Battleships of The Royal Navy

Battleships are large armored warships with a main battery consisting of large caliber guns. It dominated naval warfare in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. In 1906, the Royal Navy developed the Dreadnought-class battleship--which has revolutionized the development of other battlesips. Subsequent battleship designs influenced by HMS Dreadnought were referred to as "dreadnoughts", though the term eventually became obsolete as dreadnoughts became the only type of battleship in common use.
